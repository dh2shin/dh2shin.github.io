A Review of My Favorite Coffees

Daterra

This Brazilian coffee is a natural processed, light roasted gem. One of the earliest coffee beans among George Howell Coffee's selections, Daterra (also the farm's name) is a terrific introduction to specialty coffee if you are a beginner. Its sweetness from natural processing suits those who enjoy coffee with minimal acidity, and I particularly enjoyed its aged quality and note of vanilla. Works with pourover, but there is also a darker roast option for espresso.

Karatu AA

This Kenyan coffee is my absolute favorite. As a washed, high grown coffee, Karatu AA delivers one of the most accentuated acidities I've ever tried in coffee. This coffee emphasizes both stonefruity acidity and citrusy/orangy sweetness. In addition to these complex notes, I particularly enjoyed its long-lasting mouthfeel and aroma. Works best with a V60 from my experience.

Buy Here
Tarrazu

This Costa Rican coffee is a washed variety extremely popular across the world due to its well-bodied texture and smooth chocolaty sweetness. A fun fact about Costa Rica: it's illegal to grow coffee that isn't 100% arabica. Living upto expectations, Tarrazu delivers quality beans that are well grown and harvested. Because of its relatively low acidity and savory sweetness, Tarrazu works well with almost all brewing methods, including pourover, french-press, and espresso.

Buy Here
Gitwe Hill

This is the first coffee from Burundi I've ever had. Burundi is a small country bordering Rwanda, and the farms of Gitwe Hill only harvest cherries from about a few hundred coffee trees. By the way, Gitwe Hill means "Place of Skulls." Yikes! The coffee has intense acidity with light-to-medium body with strong notes of berries. I had a bit of trouble extracting this coffee, as the earthy, muddy notes of berries were often extracted. I also recommend cooling the coffee down a touch more than your regular coffee; it helped with reducing the earthy notes which I don't find particularly appealing. Works best with pourovers, V60 in particular.

Buy Here
Guduba Medium

This coffee is the medium-roasted version of coffees from Guduba. I find it to be quite an interesting Ethiopian coffee because of its caramely sweetness. I suspect a part of the sweetness is from the extra roasting, but the floral notes are still strong despite how more developed beans tend to have a smokier, roastier quality. Compared to the famed Yirgacheffe, George Howell Coffee's Guduba selection delivers less acidity but more sweetness. Works best with pourovers, Kalita 102 in particular.
